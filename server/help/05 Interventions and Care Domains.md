**Interventions** are therapeutic actions or lifestyle modifications that can be applied to conditions. They are organized under **care domains** based on the NEWSTART+ framework.

## Care domains

Care domains represent the pillars of lifestyle medicine. They are managed by administrators and shared across the entire team. The default care domains follow the NEWSTART+ model:

| Domain | Focus area |
|--------|-----------|
| **Nutrition** | Diet, whole foods, plant-based eating, specific nutrients |
| **Exercise** | Physical activity, movement, strength training, rehabilitation |
| **Water** | Hydration, hydrotherapy, water treatments |
| **Sunlight** | Light exposure, vitamin D, circadian rhythm |
| **Temperance** | Avoidance of harmful substances, moderation, self-control |
| **Air** | Fresh air, breathing exercises, respiratory health |
| **Rest** | Sleep, sabbath rest, stress reduction, relaxation techniques |
| **Trust in God** | Spiritual health, faith, prayer, mental well-being |

Additional care domains can be created by administrators to cover other therapeutic areas.

Each care domain has:
- A **name** and **description**
- An **icon** and **color** for visual identification
- A **sort order** controlling display position

## Interventions

An intervention is a specific therapeutic action. Examples:

- "Mediterranean Diet" (Nutrition domain)
- "30-Minute Daily Walking" (Exercise domain)
- "Cold Hydrotherapy" (Water domain)
- "Morning Sunlight Exposure" (Sunlight domain)

Each intervention has:
- A **name** and optional **description**
- A linked **care domain**
- An optional backing **document** for detailed protocols
- A **category** for sub-grouping within a domain

## Linking interventions to conditions

In the condition editor, go to the **Interventions** tab and click **Add Intervention**. You can:

1. Select an existing intervention from the list
2. Or create a new intervention on the spot

For each condition-intervention link, you can set:

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Evidence Level** | How strong the research evidence is (e.g., Strong, Moderate, Limited, Emerging) |
| **Recommendation Level** | How strongly this intervention is recommended for this condition |
| **Clinical Notes** | Free-text notes about dosing, protocols, contraindications, or implementation details |
| **Sort Order** | Display position within the condition\u2019s intervention list |

## Reordering interventions

Drag and drop interventions to reorder them within a condition. The sort order is saved automatically. You can also group interventions by care domain for clearer organization.

## Permissions

- **All team members** can view interventions and care domains
- **All team members** can create, edit, and delete interventions
- **Only administrators** can create, edit, or delete care domains
